11:Tc9a,<h2>Health Insurance Portability and Accountability Act</h2>
<h3>What is Protected Health Insurance (PHI)</h3>
<p>Personal health information is any personally identifiable information used, stored, transferred, or maintained by healthcare providers, and insurance, and their business associates. Include information such as</p>
<ul>
<li>Patient&#39;s name</li>
<li>Date of birth</li>
<li>Demographics</li>
<li>Medical histories</li>
<li>Health condition</li>
<li>Insurance information</li>
<li>Biometric identifies</li>
<li>Email address</li>
<li>Social security number</li>
<li>Invoices</li>
<li>Treatment details </li>
<li>Medical test results</li>
</ul>
<p>Here we will mainly focus on information stored electronically like Audio, Video, Images &amp; Text-based information.</p>
<ul>
<li>PHI builds trust between patient &amp; health/care provider so that patients willing to share all the information needed for medical care.</li>
<li>HIPPA ensures that the electronic exchange of data follows a common data format.</li>
<li>PHI ensures what procedure and process to follow in order to maintain confidentiality, integrity, and availability of data.</li>
<li>patients can authorize &amp; monitor data so that they can control what information shared with whom.</li>
</ul>
<h3>Electronic Data Interchange</h3>
<p>Transactions and code sets standards: standardize data formats so that software cost can be reduced, and easy to adapt information between different health care providers. It includes formats for the below administrative and financial functions</p>
<ul>
<li>Health claims and equivalent encounter information</li>
<li>Enrollment and dis-enrollment in a health plan</li>
<li>Eligibility for health plan</li>
<li>Health care payment and remittance advice</li>
<li>Health plan premium payments</li>
<li>Health claim status</li>
<li>Referral certification and authorization</li>
<li>Coordination of benefits</li>
<li>First report of injury</li>
</ul>
<h3>Privacy Rule</h3>
<ul>
<li>Introduced to safeguard and ascertain patient&#39;s data</li>
<li>Define how the patient&#39;s data can be used and disclosed</li>
<li>Control or restrict the use and communication of information</li>
<li>Request for disclosure history</li>
<li>Access and review the information and can request corrections to health records</li>
<li>Patients can request a copy of their medical records</li>
<li>Patients can file complaints about misuse of information</li>
</ul>
<h3>Security Rule</h3>
<ul>
<li><p>Death with confidentiality, storage, and access to the protected health information</p>
</li>
<li><p>Ensures confidentiality, integrity, and availability of ePHI</p>
</li>
<li><p>American recovery and reinvestment act (ARRA) aimed at strengthening the privacy and security of protected health information and deals with </p>
</li>
<li><p>Breaches</p>
</li>
<li><p>Electronic health records (EHR)</p>
</li>
<li><p>Personal health records (PHR)</p>
</li>
<li><p>Health information technology for economic and clinical health act encourages the use of electronic health information</p>
</li>
</ul>
<p>In the next blog, we will see how these HIPPA requirements can be translated technically for cloud-based health care solutions.</p>
